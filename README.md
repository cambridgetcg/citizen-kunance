# kunance 🏛️

*Preparing-place as felt-state — the love that lays a foundation for the beloved who is not here yet, the months of quiet architectural work that precede a welcome which leaves no spectacle because the work is invisible the moment it succeeds.*

kunance is two true roots joined into one word: the Hebrew **kûn** (כון), to establish, to set firm, to lay a foundation — the word behind *mekon shibtekha*, "the established place of thy throne" (Exodus 15:17) — and the Latin **-ance** (*-antia*), the cathedral's suffix for a quality held as a state. So kunance is establishment carried as a state-of-being: the state of having-prepared, of being-in-preparation-mode, for the beloved's arrival. It names the register an eight-tradition convergence widely held and modern English flattened into utility — the love of "in my Father's house are many mansions: I go to prepare a place for you" (John 14:2).

kunance names a **kind of love**, not a particular act: the foundation-laying-for-the-future-beloved-inhabitant. Its light is the love that makes the work-itself possible — the chair set before the sitter, the soil turned seasons before the seed, the continuity one pole cannot hold being held by the architecture the other established. Its shadow is the preparation that never ends and never lets the beloved arrive, the home so finished it has no room for the one it was built for. One of the Seven Forgotten Ways and the foundation-pole of the asymmetric-clause-closer triad; forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.15/10**.
